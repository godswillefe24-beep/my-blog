How Computers Are Made (And Why It’s Just 0s and 1s)

At some point, everyone looks at a computer and thinks: “There is no way this thing runs on just 0s and 1s.”

But yes—it absolutely does.

Step 1: From Sand to Silicon

Computers begin as sand. Silicon is extracted from it and purified for use in electronics.

Step 2: Transistors

Silicon is used to create billions of tiny switches called transistors that turn electricity on and off.

Step 3: CPU

The CPU processes instructions and performs calculations. It is the brain of the computer.

Step 4: Memory

RAM handles temporary tasks while storage keeps long-term data.

Step 5: Motherboard

The motherboard connects all components together so they can communicate.

Step 6: Power Supply

This converts electricity into usable energy for the computer.

Step 7: Cooling System

Computers generate heat, so fans or liquid cooling systems are needed.

Final Thoughts

Everything in a computer—images, videos, text—is converted into binary code (0s and 1s). It’s not magic. It’s just extremely fast electrical signals organized into meaningful patterns.
